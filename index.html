Summary

Fat, Sick & Nearly Dead (2010) follows Australian Joe Cross on a transformative 60-day juice fast across the U.S., aiming to shed weight and cure his chronic illness. Along the way, he meets people suffering similar health issues and introduces them to the juicing lifestyle. The documentary captures both the physical journey and emotional revelations as Joe attempts to regain control of his life and inspire others to do the same.

Review Verdict

The documentary is visually engaging and emotionally impactful, offering a compelling narrative of self-transformation. However, it lacks scientific backing, with minimal consultation from healthcare professionals. While inspirational, it oversimplifies complex health issues, leaving questions about the practicality and safety of prolonged juicing unanswered.

In-Depth Analysis

The film focuses heavily on emotional appeal, emphasizing before-and-after transformations.
